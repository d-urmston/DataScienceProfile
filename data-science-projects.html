Research Question

In football, it is believed that the height of central defenders significantly impacts their heading ability. While there are exceptions there is a belief within the industry that this height advantage allows them to excel in winning aerial duels, particularly during high crosses and corner kicks. So is it a case of the taller the defenders, the better they are at heading the ball?

Introduction/Project background

This project explores the world of football scouting and changes in the game. This analysis could be used by football clubs to assist in recruitment and could offer insight into how the game changes over time.

Data Collection

Source: Player profile data was pulled from FIFA 23 complete player dataset (Kaggle, 2023) and enriched with FIFA World Ranking 1992-2024 (Kaggle, 2023)

Variables: Critical variables include date of birth, heading ability, height, position

Data Preparation and cleaning

The data for this data science project was cleansed in a few different ways.

Removing columns

Selectively eliminating unnecessary columns or features from a dataset, can streamline work, reduce noise, and focus on relevant information. This practice enhances the overall quality of the data, making subsequent analyses more accurate and efficient.

Filters

To enhance the focus of the dataset onto the centre backs filtering was required.

The column containing positions was used to create a single column to filter on.

This was done by using the formular =LEFT(G2,2). This will pull in the primary position for the player as this project is focused on centre backs (CB) only.

Figure 1.image of football player dataset

Standardizing Country Names

In the process of data cleansing, one critical task is linking countries to a comprehensive conversion table. This table contains various naming conventions for different countries, ensuring consistency and accuracy across datasets. Here s how this step was approached.

Conversion Table Creation

As part of the data cleansing workflow, analysts construct a conversion table. This table maps alternative country names (such as abbreviations, variations, or localized spellings) to a standardized format.

For instance, USA might map to United States, UK to United Kingdom, and so forth.

Joining with Main Data

The next step involves joining this conversion table with the main dataset. Specifically, link it to the nationality field.

By doing so, they create a unified reference point for country names, regardless of how they were initially input.

Case Consistency

To future-proof the data, analysts apply an uppercase transformation to the linked country names. This ensures that regardless of whether a user enters united states, United States, or USA, the data remains consistent.

In summary, this meticulous process of linking countries and standardizing their names ensures data accuracy, facilitates cross-referencing, and prepares the dataset for robust analysis

Data Analysis

Linear Regression

For the analysis linear regression was used to quantify the relationship between height and heading ability. In summary, simple linear regression allows us to understand and model the association between height and heading ability effectively.

Figure 3.image of regression statistics

 

As proven by the regression statistics the correlation coefficient (Multiple R) is approximately 0.19. This value suggests a weak linear relationship between the height of defenders and their heading ability.

The coefficient of determination (R Square) is around 0.04. This indicates that only a small proportion of the variance in the heading ability can be predicted from the players height.

The standard error is approximately 8.35. Larger standard errors imply more variability around the estimated regression line. As seen in the image below.

Figure 4. image of regression plot

 

In summary this piece of analysis would lead me to the conclusion that there is no significant relationship between the height of a defender and the heading ability of the player.

Time series

The next piece of analysis completed was a time series exploring the height changes over time. It was decided a time series line graph would be best placed to analyse the trend up or down over the course of years and seasons. Now that the regression has proved the lack of a relationship emphasis on teams having taller centre backs could potentially prove to be less important.

 

Figure 5. Image of time series graph

As you can see over time the average height of centre backs has dropped from approximately 183cm for players born in 1990 and reduced to approximately 177cm on average for those born in 2002. This drop3% could be down to various reasons.

Potential Theories

Playing Style: Modern football emphasizes agility, quick transitions, and ball-playing abilities.

Tactical Changes: Teams adopted zonal marking, pressing, and high defensive lines.

Technical Skills: Centre backs now need to distribute the ball effectively, participate in build-up play, and even venture forward during attacks.

Scouting Trends: Clubs started valuing versatility over sheer height, seeking defenders who could adapt to various situations.

This analysis could be completed in the future enriching my original dataset with new datasets around pitch data etc could lead to interesting analysis in the future.

World Heatmap

The last piece of analysis completed was around the nationality of the players.

Using a COUNT formula to determine which countries produce the most defenders. The darker the colour the higher the concentration of defenders for that area.

This piece of analysis would be valuable to scouts as they could focus their scouting efforts on nations that tend to produce more central defenders. It also gives a very fast and easy to digest view of the concentration of central defenders across the globe.

Figure 6. world map showing hotspots of defenders

 

For this analysis it was decided a single colour would be used to emphasize the statistical hotspots. Different colour pallets were also considered for this visualisation. However certain colours can be indistinguishable from each other for people with colour blindness. In fact, Masataka Okabe (Jikei Medical School) and Kei Ito (University of Tokyo) developed a colour palette known as Colour Universal Design (CUD) (2002, Masataka Okabe, Kei Ito) This palette consists of eight colours that are easily distinguishable by both colour blind and non-colourblind individuals. It has gained worldwide support and is the default colour scale in Claus Wilke s book Fundamentals of Data Visualization however due to restraints in the business it was decided that a single colour with differing shades should be used as these are also easily distinguishable by both colour blind and non-colourblind individuals.

 

Discussions/Recommendations

There s an old adage in boxing that a good big man always beats a good little man , meaning if all other aspects (talent, age, fitness, etc) are equal, the bigger man will always prevail. Traditionally, similar assumptions have been made about centre-backs, with height often considered a prerequisite for being effective in the role. (2022, Sarah Shephard). In light of the preliminary research and analysis, we recommend that football teams shift their focus away from center-backs height, which has traditionally received significant attention. Our findings suggest that other factors may be more influential in optimizing performance and injury prevention.

Future Project Improvements

Introduce More Variables

Feature Engineering: Identify additional relevant features (variables) that can enhance model performance. For example passing ability, tackles made per game etc. Hundreds of different variables could be used to enhance this analysis and research.

Expand range over time

Longitudinal Data: Collect data over an extended period. This allows for trend analysis and capturing seasonality.

Higher Granularity: Collect data more frequently (e.g., weekly instead of monthly) to capture finer variations of the different variables which would show how certain dynamics may change over the course of a season.

Outliers and Extremes: Ensure the dataset includes extreme cases (e.g., exceptional performances, rare events).

Introduce a Wider Group

Expand Variables: Include wider groups such as lower leagues. Youth team etc.

Segmentation: Analyse subgroups within the wider audience. International, Pro, Semi-Pro, Amateur, Youth Player etc).

Sentiment Analysis: Incorporate sentiment data (e.g social media sentiment) related to the audience s perception of a player s performance and their perceived personality

Ethical Considerations

Bias and Fairness: Assess and mitigate bias in the data and model predictions.

Privacy Considerations: Consider how sensitive data is handled, especially when dealing with personal information such as wages or details around youth team players.
